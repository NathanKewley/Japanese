# い And な Adjective Conjugations

The way adjectives are conjugated into differen forms differes for `い-Adjectives` And `な-Adjectives`. Each of the conjugation types are explained with a table at the bottom providing examples of all cases.

See [い-Adjectives-And-な-Adjectives](い-Adjectives-And-な-Adjectives.md) for how to identify what type an adjective is.

If you have an adjective that needs to be conjugated multiple times (i.e. Negative Past) there is an order in which the conjugations must happen:

1. Negation
1. Past

## Negation

### い-Adjectives Negation

Once you have identified an adjective as being an `い-Adjectives` negating it becomes easy.

Drop the final `い` from the base adjective and replace it with `くない`.

So `あついです` becomes `あつくないです`.

### な-Adjectives Negation

Once you have identified an adjective as being an `な-Adjectives` negating it becomes easy.

The base of the adjective remains the same, however `です` is replaced with `じゃありません`.

So `しずかです` becomes `しずかじゃありません`.

## Past Tense

### い-Adjectives Past Tense

Changing an `い-Adjective` to past tense is straight forward. the final `い` is dropped and replaced with `かった`.

So `ちかいです` becomes `ちかかったです`.

You can also mix conjugations to make `negative past`. In this case the conjugation order must be followed and the adjective is first conjugated to negative form, then the negative form is conjugated to make `negative past`.

So `ちかいです` becomes `ちかくないです` and finally `ちかくなかったです`.

### な-Adjectives Past Tense

Changing a `な-Adjectives` to past tense is simple. you appends `でした` to the end where you would normall append `です`

So `しずかです` becomes `しずかでした`.

As with `い-Adjective` you can mix conjugations following the same conjugation order. So to conjugate `な-Adjectives` to `negative past` you need to first conjugate to negative, then the negative form is conjugated to past.

So `しずかです` becomes `しずかじゃありません` and finally `しずかじゃありませんでした`

## Exceptions

The exceptions to adjective types have thier own rules for conjugation types, hence this is treated as a seperate section.

### いいです

The negative form of `いいです` is simply `よくないです`. You just have to remember this. 🙃
