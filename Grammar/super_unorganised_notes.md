# Super Unorganised Notes

## 〜んだよ

「〜んだ」is the spoken form of 「〜のだ」— adds explanation or emphasis. 

しつこいんだよ = "(I'm telling you) he's so persistent!"

Masculine speech. Women say 「しつこいんだもん」or 「しつこいのよ」instead.

## よ

「よ」is a sentence-ending particle that pushes your assertion toward the listener


## 〜め

「め」 is a suffix expressing contempt, anger  attached to the end of a noun.

It's used to look down on or insult someone.

卑怯者め
You coward!

バカめ
You idiot!

この野郎め
You bastard!

## 〜んだ

「どんな修行を叩き込んだ」どんなしゅぎょうをたたきこんだ
"Just what kind of training did you pound into her?!" 

「〜んだ」is the spoken form of 「〜のだ」— adds explanation or emphasis.

 The speaker isn't really expecting an answer — they're expressing shock or disbelief.

Masculine. A woman would more likely say 「叩き込んだの？」.

## 〜ものだ

「ものだ」— General Tendency / Universal Truth
Used to express something that is naturally so, generally true, or "that's just how things are."

## 〜とする

「強固なものとする」
to make robust

「〜とする」≈ 「〜にする」/ "to make it / to hereby declare it as" Used to formally state a goal or resolution.

## ひでえ

「ひどい」→「ひでえ」— a sound shift from Edo/Tokyo working-class dialect.

Standard
Rough form
ひどい
ひでえ　　terrible 　

すごい
すげえ　　amazing 

やばい
やべえ　　crazy 

でかい
でけえ　　huge 

い-adjectives: 「〜い」→「〜え」 at the end.

## 〜てやる

「助けてやる」
「〜てやる」is the rough version of 「〜てあげる」("to do something for someone").
Expression


助けてあげる
polite 

助けてやる
rough 

助けてやろう
dramatic resolve — "I will save you"
