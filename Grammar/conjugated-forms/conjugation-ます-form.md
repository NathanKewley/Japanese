# Conjugations ます Verb Form

`ます Form` can be thought of as the polite form of verbs. Generally this is how you should speak with an aquaitance or anyone of higher social status. Just use common sense and keep in mind that Japanese is very likely to generally be more formal in language than your language is.

`ます Form` itself has variations for present, past, negative and negative past. All of which are covered here.

> [!WARNING]
> The `ます Form` is often the first thing someone will learn when learning Japanese like in the case with the `みんなにほんご` textbook. However I stongly believe its better to learn the `Dictionary form` of words first and that is what is assumed here.

## Preparing for ます Form

To conjugate Verbs into the `ます Form` you must first conjugate the `dictionary form` of the verb into the [Stem form](verb-types.md).

## Present and Future tense

Present tense `ます Form` is created by appending `ます` to the `Stem Form` of the Verb.

| Dictionary Form | Stem Form | ます Present |
| --------------- | --------- | ------------ |
| たべる          | たべ       | たべます     |
| たべす          | はなし     | はなします   |

## Negative Tense

Negative form takes present `ます Form` and swaps `ます` for `ません`

| Dictionary Form | Stem Form | ます Negative |
| --------------- | --------- | ------------ |
| たべる          | たべ       | たべません    |
| たべす          | はなし     | はなしません  |

## Past Tense

Past form takes present `ます Form` and swaps `ます` for `ました`

| Dictionary Form | Stem Form | ます Past    |
| --------------- | --------- | ------------ |
| たべる          | たべ       | たべました    |
| たべす          | はなし     | はなしました  |

## Past Negative Tense

Past negative form takes the negative form and appends `でした` to the ending `ません`

| Dictionary Form | Stem Form | ます Past Negative |
| --------------- | --------- | ------------------ |
| たべる          | たべ       | たべませんでした    |
| たべす          | はなし     | はなしませんでした  |
