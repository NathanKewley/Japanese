# ~なさそう - Doesn't Seem Likely To

[<- Back](../../README.md)

`~なさそう` is attached to the **ます Stem** of a verb to indicate that something **doesn't appear likely to happen** or that someone **doesn't seem likely to do something**. It expresses the speaker's judgment based on what they observe or know.

## Forms and Usage

`~なさそう` is attached to the ます Stem of a verb.

| Type            | Form          | Example (食べる → eat) |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------------------|
| Plain Non-past  | ～なさそう      | 食べなさそう           |
| Polite Non-past | ～なさそうです   | 食べなさそうです       |
| Plain Past      | ～なさそうだった | 食べなさそうだった      |
| Polite Past     | ～なさそうでした | 食べなさそうでした      |

## Formation

| Verb Type  | Rule                                   | Example               |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Ichidan    | Remove る, add ～なさそう                | 食べる → 食べなさそう    |
| Godan      | Change to the ます Stem, add ～なさそう  | 行く → 行きなさそう      |
| Irregular  | する → しなさそう<br>来る → 来(こ)なさそう | 勉強する → 勉強しなさそう |

## Examples

いつもは興味なさそうなのに。
You usually don't seem interested in things like this.

今日は雨が降りなさそうだ。  
It doesn't look like it will rain today.

彼は来なさそうです。  
It doesn't seem like he'll come.

美味しくなさそう。 
It does not seem tasty.

## Notes

- `～なさそう` expresses an **impression or expectation**, not certainty.
- It means **"doesn't seem likely to..."** rather than simply **"doesn't..."**.
- It is based on the speaker's observations or assumptions.
- For a definite negative statement, use the **ない Form** instead.
