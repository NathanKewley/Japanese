# いきます　と　きます

[<- Back](../../README.md)

* `いきます` -（行きます）To suggest going/gone somewhere the speaker is not currently.
* `きます` -（来ます）To talk about coming to where the speaker currently is

The term used depends on the perspective of the speaker, therefore いきます is used when the speaker is talking about going to somewhere they are not currently. きます is used when the speaker is talking about coming to the location they are currently at.

## いきます Example 1

```text
きのう　コンビニ　へ　いきました

Yesterday I went to the Conviniance Store.
```

`いきます` is used in the past tense as you are stating you went somewhere `different` to where you currently are.

## いきます Example 2

```text
Aさん：こんばん、いっしょうに　いざかや　へ　いきませんか
Bさん：いいですね。いきましょう

A : Do you want to go to an izakaya this evening?
B : Sounds good, lets go.
```

`いきます` is used here in the negative tense to form a question of shall we go somewhere `different` to where the speaker is now.

## きます Example 1

```text
まいにち　でんしゃ　で　がっこう　へ　きます

Everyday I ride my bike to school.
```

`きます` is used here indicating that the place the speaker is talking about is the place they currently are, in this case they are at `がっこう` and talking about `がっこう`.

## きます Example 2

```text
A : まいにち　なんじ　に　がっこう　へ　きますか
B : １２じ　に　きます
A : なに　で　きますか
B : じてんしゃ　で　きます

A : What time do you get to school every day?
B : At 12 o'clock.
A : How do you get to school?
B : I ride a bike.
```

Once again `きます` is used here indicating that the place the speaker is talking about is the place they currently are, in this case they are at `がっこう` and talking about `がっこう`.
