# と Particle

The `と` particle has multiple uses.

## "AND"

## Quoting Particle

The `と` particle can be used to mark a quote. Like all particles it immmediatly follows what it modifies, in this case it `MUST` come directly after what is being quoted. At this point the quote and the `と` particle are joined together and treated as a single `Noun`.

`「面白い　ことが　ない」と　アリスは　言った` (Alice said "There is nothing intersting")

The `と` particle can also be used in this way to quote a sound effect.

`うさぎは　ピョンピョンと　はしり` (The rabbit ran in a way that make the "sound effect" of a small create jumping around)

> [NOTE]
> Japanese used `「」` bracers as quotation markers.

## Enacting on Something

This probably should be considered as under the same category as using `と` as the `Quoting Particle` as it indictes that the `Quoted` action is being enacted upon.

`山に　登ろう　とする` (I will act on my will to climb this mountain)
