# よう Volitional

The `Volitinoal Form` as usual has multiple uses in Japanese.

## Proposing and Inviting

## Expressing your will

This is expressing the fact you will `try` to do something, this implied doubt that it is possible to do. So maybe you want to climb a mountain by you are not sure its possible.

* 山に　登ろう　とする (I will act on my will to climb this mountain).

> [!NOTE]
> The `と` particle used in the example is using `と` to indicate that an action is enacted upon. See [と](../particles/と.md)
