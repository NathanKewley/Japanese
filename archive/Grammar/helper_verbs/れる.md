# れる - Receptive Helper Verb

This is also sometimes called the `Passive Form`. Verbs are conjugated into the `れる Form` to indicate the verb was done to the subject of the sentance. This can take the form `<Subjectが><Verb-れる-Form>.

* `田中さんが　のまれた` (Tanaka got drunk) <sup>1</sup>

Importantly this is not limited to people:

* `ビールが　のまれた` (The beer got drunk)

You can also use this in conjuction with the [に](../particles/に.md) `Target of an Action` function to indicate the actor performing/causing the change:

* `ビールが　ねこに　のまれた` (The beer was drunk by the cat! 😱)

<sub>1. (酔う/よう - Godan Verb)</sub>
