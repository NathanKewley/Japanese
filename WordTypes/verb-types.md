# Verb Types

[<- BACK](./README.md)

`Verbs` are words that describe an action, there are 3 types of `Verbs` in Japanese. `Ichidan`, `Godan` and `Irregular`. This page will cover the different types of Verbs.

For details on Conjugations please see [conjugations2](../conjugations2.md)

## Ichidan

`Ichidan` verbs are also sometimes refered to as `1-step` and `م‚‹` verbs.

The dictionary form of all `Ichidan` verbs end with `م‚‹`. However not every verb that ends in `م‚‹` is an `Ichidan` verb. There is no set rule or logic to this... you just have to lean which verbs ending in `م‚‹` are `Ichidan` or `Godan`. (Love me some ambiguity).

### Ichidan Examples

* مپںمپ¹م‚‹ (To Eat)
* مپٹمپچم‚‹ (To wake up)
* مپ؟م‚‹ (To see)

## Godan

`Godan` verbs are also sometimes refered to as `5-step` and `مپ†` verbs.

If a verb does not end in `م‚‹` it is deffiantly a `Godan` Verb. If a verb ends in `م‚‹` it **MIGHT** be a `Godan` Verb. There is no rule for this, you just have to know which are which.

### Godan Examples

* مپ‹مپڈ (To Write)
* مپچم‚‹ (To Cut)
* مپ¯مپھمپ™ (To Talk)

## Irregular Verbs

There are 2 irregular verbs (2 too many) which you just have to learn.

* `مپ™م‚‹` (To Do)
* `مپڈم‚‹` (To Come)
